About Me

An introduction to myself, how I got into computer science, and my career goals in the future.

How I Got Into Computer Science

Since I was a kid, I was always interested in the way electronics and digital circuitury worked. My grandfather, in fact, was an electrical engineer and brought me into the world of electronics and computers. Eventually, I obtained my first computer which was an Apple IIe. It also became the computer I would use to learn programming using Apple BASIC, which is the "grandfather" of Visual Basic which people still use today.

During my middle school and high school years, I took typewriting lessons, and with it, learned how to program in Java, and do Web Design using HTML and CSS. I was also introduced to open-source, using Ubuntu Linux at home at a time when the linux kernel was just beginning to gain popularity for programmers. By the time I got to college, I was more than convinced that this was the field I wanted to get into. Before I knew it, I was completeing my prerequsities to complete my Mathematics minor and first projects in old-school ANSI C, and then later useing Java 6 several years back.

Thanks to the background I had previously with C++, I learned OOP useing C++11, as well as Data Structures and Algorithms which was interesting and very challenging due to the nature of the language. I then took courses useing a dynamic range of languages - from MIPS and X86 Assembly to even Ada (which I made a report on while takeing a Programming Languages course). So far as a Senior-level student, I have completed courses and projects discussing all of the above, plus: Compiler Construction, Computer Architecture, Operating Systems Design, Discrete Mathematics, Engineering Statistics, Mobile Programming with Android, and Database Design and Development.

As of Fall 2019, I will be completing courses and projects discussing Expert Systems, Web-Applications Programming, Theory of Computation, and a Software Engineering practicum.

I am also takeing several certifications outside of my degree through DataQuest on Data Science with Python, you can find my progress there through this link.
